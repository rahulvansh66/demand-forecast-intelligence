# Executive Business Requirements Document Design

## Project Context

This design specification outlines the structure and content for an executive business requirements document for Walmart's AI-powered demand forecasting copilot. The document targets senior leadership for investment approval of a $1-5M proof of concept over 6-12 months, with clear scale strategy to enterprise deployment.

## Document Objective

Create a compelling business case that demonstrates:
- Clear ROI with conservative projections
- Risk-mitigated pilot approach (3 stores, 3 categories)
- Proven path to enterprise-wide value ($50M+ annual impact)
- Comprehensive value proposition addressing all four business drivers: revenue protection, cost optimization, competitive differentiation, and operational efficiency

## Document Structure: ROI-First Approach

### Section 1: Executive Summary (2 pages)
**Purpose:** Hook leadership with the business case upfront
**Content Requirements:**
- **Business Challenge:** Quantify current demand forecasting limitations and associated costs
  - Lost sales revenue from stockouts (focus on high-margin categories)
  - Excess inventory carrying costs and markdown losses
  - Manual forecasting labor inefficiencies
- **Proposed Solution:** AI-powered demand forecasting copilot using M5 dataset methodology
  - Three core capabilities: demand forecasting, behavior profiling, GenAI insights
  - Proven academic foundation with business-ready implementation
- **Investment Ask:** $1-5M over 6-12 months for proof of concept
- **Expected Returns:** 15-25% forecast accuracy improvement, quantified savings potential
- **Proof of Concept Scope:** 3 stores, 3 categories (Food, Hobbies, Households)
- **Scale Vision:** Clear path to 4,700+ store enterprise deployment

**Key Requirements:**
- One compelling statistic about current inventory costs as hook
- ROI projection with payback period under 18 months
- Risk-mitigated approach emphasizing pilot-first methodology

### Section 2: Financial Impact Analysis
**Purpose:** Detailed ROI calculations for investment justification
**Content Requirements:**
- **Current State Costs Analysis:**
  - Annual inventory carrying costs for excess stock
  - Lost sales revenue from stockouts (emphasize high-margin impact)
  - Manual forecasting and planning labor costs
  - Markdown losses from overstock situations
  - Quantified opportunity cost vs. competitors

- **Projected Savings (Proof of Concept Phase):**
  - 15-25% reduction in forecast error → inventory optimization savings
  - 5-10% reduction in stockouts → revenue protection
  - 20-30% reduction in manual forecasting time → labor cost savings
  - Customer satisfaction score improvements → long-term value

- **Investment Breakdown:**
  - Technology development and ML infrastructure: 60%
  - Data engineering and system integration: 25%
  - Change management and training: 15%

- **ROI Timeline and Projections:**
  - Break-even: Month 8-12 of pilot
  - 3-year NPV projection with scale assumptions
  - Sensitivity analysis (conservative, expected, optimistic scenarios)

**Key Requirements:**
- Every dollar spent tied to specific business outcome
- Conservative estimates to build credibility
- Clear methodology for calculating savings

### Section 3: Proof of Concept Scope
**Purpose:** Define specific deliverables for 6-12 month validation
**Content Requirements:**
- **Target Categories (3 total):**
  - **Food:** High-frequency purchases, clear seasonal/event patterns
  - **Hobbies:** Discretionary spending, seasonal peaks (holidays, summer)
  - **Households:** Essential items, steady demand with seasonal variations
  - Rationale: Diverse demand patterns test all system capabilities

- **Store Selection (3 total):**
  - Performance mix: high-performing and average-performing locations
  - Regional representation for seasonality and demand pattern testing

- **Core Capabilities Delivered:**
  - 7-day demand forecasting with 85%+ accuracy target
  - Automated demand behavior profiling (trend, seasonality, variability, movement type)
  - GenAI business insights for inventory managers
  - Integration with existing replenishment and inventory systems

- **Success Criteria:**
  - Quantified forecast accuracy improvement vs. current methods
  - Measurable inventory turn improvements
  - User adoption rates by store managers and planners
  - System performance benchmarks (uptime, response time)

**Key Requirements:**
- Ultra-focused scope for maximum learning
- Testable across all three core system capabilities
- Clear validation of enterprise-scale potential

### Section 4: Success Metrics & Timeline
**Purpose:** Trackable milestones and KPIs for executive oversight
**Content Requirements:**
- **Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)**
  - Data integration and historical analysis completion
  - ML model training and validation on M5 dataset
  - Milestone: 80%+ accuracy on historical data validation
  - Success Metric: Error-free processing of 3 stores' historical data

- **Phase 2: Pilot Deployment (Months 4-6)**
  - Live deployment across 3 stores and 3 categories
  - Real-time forecasting and behavior profiling operational
  - Milestone: First live 7-day forecast generation
  - Success Metric: 85%+ forecast accuracy vs. actual sales

- **Phase 3: Business Impact Validation (Months 7-12)**
  - Full operational integration with store processes
  - GenAI insights driving inventory decisions
  - Milestone: Measurable business outcomes achieved
  - Success Metrics:
    - 15-20% reduction in forecast error
    - 10-15% improvement in inventory turns
    - 5-8% reduction in stockouts
    - 80%+ user satisfaction from store managers

- **Go/No-Go Decision Framework (Month 9):**
  - Clear criteria for scaling to enterprise deployment
  - ROI validation and business case confirmation
  - Technical performance and user adoption thresholds

**Key Requirements:**
- Monthly executive dashboard reporting
- Objective, measurable criteria for success
- Clear decision points for investment continuation

### Section 5: Scale Strategy
**Purpose:** Enterprise deployment roadmap and investment planning
**Content Requirements:**
- **Scale Path Framework:**
  - Wave 1: 50 stores (6 months post-pilot)
  - Wave 2: 500 stores regional rollout (12 months post-pilot)
  - Wave 3: 4,700+ stores enterprise deployment (24 months post-pilot)

- **Category Expansion Strategy:**
  - Proven categories first: Food, Hobbies, Households
  - High-impact expansion: General Merchandise
  - Complex categories: Apparel, Electronics (after core capabilities proven)

- **Infrastructure Scaling Requirements:**
  - Cloud-native auto-scaling architecture
  - Data pipeline capacity planning for enterprise load
  - Integration touchpoints with existing Walmart systems

- **Investment Scaling Model:**
  - Pilot: $1-5M (current request)
  - Wave 1: Additional $3-8M
  - Full Enterprise: Total $15-25M over 3 years
  - Self-funding model: Each wave funds next through demonstrated ROI

- **Strategic Value Proposition:**
  - Full deployment target: $50M+ annual impact
  - Market differentiation through AI-driven inventory optimization
  - Competitive advantage in customer experience and operational efficiency

**Key Requirements:**
- Clear financial planning for each scale phase
- Risk-mitigated expansion approach
- Compelling enterprise-wide value proposition

### Section 6: Risk Mitigation
**Purpose:** Proactive risk management to build executive confidence
**Content Requirements:**
- **Technical Risk Management:**
  - Data Quality: Validation pipelines, fallback procedures
  - Model Performance: Continuous monitoring, human oversight protocols
  - Integration: Phased approach, extensive testing, rollback capabilities

- **Business Risk Mitigation:**
  - User Adoption: Training programs, change management, gradual transition
  - Operational Disruption: Parallel operations, manager override capabilities
  - ROI Shortfall: Conservative projections, multiple metrics, pivot strategies

- **Strategic Risk Considerations:**
  - Competitive Response: First-mover advantage, proprietary advantages
  - Technology Evolution: Modular architecture, cloud-native scalability
  - Compliance: Data governance, audit-ready processes

- **Contingency Planning:**
  - Clear exit criteria and procedures if pilot underperforms
  - Alternative vendor evaluation pathways
  - Gradual rollback without operational disruption

- **Success Amplification:**
  - Executive steering committee with clear sponsorship
  - Regular stakeholder communication and change champions
  - Quick wins strategy to build momentum

**Key Requirements:**
- Address typical AI/ML project concerns
- Demonstrate thoughtful planning without being overly cautious
- Balance speed-to-value with risk management

## Implementation Approach

### Document Creation Process
1. **Content Development:** Use project technical documentation as foundation
2. **Financial Modeling:** Create detailed ROI calculations with conservative assumptions
3. **Executive Review:** Incorporate Walmart-specific business context and priorities
4. **Stakeholder Validation:** Ensure alignment with technical and operational teams

### Success Criteria for Document
- Executive leadership can make informed investment decision
- Clear understanding of pilot scope, timeline, and expected outcomes
- Compelling business case with quantified ROI projections
- Risk-mitigated approach that builds confidence in enterprise-scale potential

### Next Steps
After document approval, transition to detailed implementation planning covering:
- Technical architecture and development roadmap
- Data engineering and integration requirements
- Project team structure and resource allocation
- Change management and training program design
